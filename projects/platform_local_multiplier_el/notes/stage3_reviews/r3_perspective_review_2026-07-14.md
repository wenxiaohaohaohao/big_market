## Review Body

### Identity-versus-mechanism verdict

The equation omega = (1-s)rho_A + s rho_P is an accounting identity. The economic mechanism starts when CES demand makes s respond to integration, Assumption 2 fixes short-run incidence while allowing elastic local production, and the local-income fixed point makes induced spending depend on Y. Under that closure, recurring leakage and the marginal peak are model implications. They are not structural platform conduct or policy effects outside the closure.

### Object-to-domain mapping

| Object | Model meaning | Clean interpretation | Unsupported stronger interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| z | Exogenous integration intensity lowering platform price | Reduced-form access or trade-cost shock | Endogenous pricing, bargaining, pass-through, or relocation |
| s | Gross retail-expenditure share of channel P | Channel-composition margin | Adoption, users, transactions, sellers, or standard IO market share |
| rho_c | Local factor-and-ownership income per gross dollar | Spatial income incidence | Merchant revenue, wage share, commission, tax base, or welfare |
| omega | Expenditure-weighted average incidence | Compositional local retention | A structural parameter invariant to seller mix or fulfillment |
| B | Autonomous local value added | Reduced-form export-base income | Endogenous entry, relocation, or industrialization |
| G | Outside-funded nominal retail spending | External private-demand injection | Government spending or balanced-budget policy |
| Y | Aggregate local income | Regional nominal income | Distributional welfare, employment, or tax revenue |
| R | Conditional consumption-equivalent index | Price-versus-income comparison | Full social welfare or policy sufficient statistic |

### Transferable insight

When substitute spending channels differ in the fraction of gross expenditure that becomes local income, a composition shock changes both first-round spatial incidence and the coefficient governing subsequent spending rounds. Nominal income can therefore move differently from consumer prices, and the marginal retention loss need not be largest when the low-retention channel dominates. This logic can transfer to chain versus independent retail, local versus remote procurement, and alternative fulfillment arrangements if the incidence mapping is explicit.

### Scope limits

The paper cannot identify optimal platform fees, platform market power, merchant entry or exit, logistics employment, tax collection, public budgets, heterogeneous incidence, or full welfare. Regional readers can use it as a benchmark. Trade readers should not treat it as a relocation model; platform readers should not treat it as strategic IO; public-finance readers should not call the G multiplier a fiscal multiplier; policy readers can use it diagnostically but not to rank policies.

### Actionable revisions

1. State that weighted retention is the accounting component and that Assumption 2 plus the fixed point supplies the feedback mechanism.
2. Clarify that s is a gross expenditure share, rho_c is local factor-and-ownership income per gross dollar, and G is private external demand.
3. State that platform/trade applications require exogenous price pass-through and fixed short-run incidence; fiscal and policy applications need taxes, budgets, heterogeneity, and complete welfare.
4. Preserve the reduced-form scope rather than turning the letter into a full platform or spatial GE model.
