## Review Body

### Strongest contribution

The paper combines CES channel substitution with a local-income fixed point in which the channel retention share applies to every induced retail-spending round. Its most letter-worthy result is the interior maximum of the marginal retention drag below a one-half platform expenditure share. The propagation denominator shifts the familiar CES substitution peak. Proposition 1's distinction between a local-income injection and gross outside-funded retail spending supplies the conceptual foundation.

### Desk-review risk

The main risk is salience rather than correctness. An editor must see immediately why the multiplier comparison, finite sign regions, and peak are more than algebraic restatements of the closure. The closest analytical benchmark is not named as directly as the empirical applications. The abstract can also be read too broadly unless the short-run incidence and incomplete-welfare boundaries are made more visible.

### Actionable requests

1. Name the closest analytical benchmark after the contribution paragraph and identify exactly what it lacks: an expenditure-share-dependent retention coefficient applied in every induced round.
2. Make the conclusion restate the distinction between an injection of already formed local income and gross external retail spending.
3. Move a compact scope boundary earlier: channel incidence shares are short-run primitives and the real-income measure is not full welfare.
4. Verify whether the Li working paper has a stable repository record or publication status.

No request requires a larger model.
